tl;dr

I spoke at a LondonR hootenanny1 (in-person!) about how to create your own simple Twitter bot powered by GitHub Actions and {rtweet}, just like my @londonmapbot creation.

The mapbotverse

I created a Twitter bot called @londonmapbot. It uses the {rtweet} package by Mike Kearney to tweet out a random satellite image of Greater London via Mapbox, scheduled and executed by GitHub Actions.

I’ve written about this before:

  • A Twitter bot with {rtweet} and GitHub Actions (original post)
  • Mapping londonmapbot tweets with {leaflet}
  • What colour is London?

I’ve noticed a number of other projects have used or developed the londonmapbot code, or else are inspired by it. I’ve created a Twitter List containing the ones I know about, which—in earnest—I’ve called ‘the mapbotverse’.

It includes bots that tweet maps more cleverly, or do something else, like:

  • Roberto Jiménez’s @esmapbot, which tweets images of Spain sampled from within a geojson of the country’s borders
  • Matt Kerlogue’s @narrowbotR, which tweets canal and river locations with geographically-coincident Flickr images, which have been rated for photo quality (!)
  • Oscar Baruffa’s @BigBookofR, which tweets out a random section of the excellent Big Book of R resource

You too can fork the source for londonmapbot on GitHub, or click the green ‘use this template’ button in the repo to begin your own mapbot. Or can you can create something from scratch. Let me know what you get up to.
